# Stock Market Prediction:

Choosing a model for stock market prediction involve balancing meaningful trends and random market volatility because financial data can change over time.


## Tree-Based & Ensemble Models
Examples: Random Forest, XGBoost, LightGBM.

How they work: These use thousands of "if-then" decision rules to map features (like volume, RSI, or moving averages) to a price.

Trade-offs:

- Pros: Excellent at capturing non-linear relationships and handling tabular data. They are robust to outliers (like a sudden one-day price spike).

- Cons: They cannot extrapolate. If a stock reaches an all-time high, a tree-based model literally cannot predict a price higher than it has seen in the training data.
